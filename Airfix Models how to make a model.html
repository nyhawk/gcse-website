How to build an Airfix Model

Here, I will show you the way that I build Airfix planes. There are many other sets made by Airfix but I have only made aircraft. I am building the Mitsubishi A6M2b Zero starter set.


Here is what the box looks like. On the front, there is an illustration of the plane and on the back there are the painting instructions.




This kit contains

  • 4 paint pots
  • A brush
  • Glue
  • 4 sprues
  • Transfers or decals
  • Instructions

The first thing that I always do when building a model is read though the instructions and paint any parts that need painting before they are glued so they are ready to be assembled.




I clamped the two sides of the main plane shape together with the cockpit inside to stop the sides coming apart and causing a gap. It was very difficult to put the cockpit in the right place in this plane and it is important that it is glued in exactly the right place as if it isn't, the plane will not fit together properly. It was so difficult that I forgot to take a picture of the cockpit before I stuck it in!




This is what the cockpit should look like. These pictures are from another tutorial for a similar airfix plane kit. You can watch this tutorial below;






Here is a picture of part of the cockpit. You can see the very small and fragile transfers that are inside that I managed to put on without breaking them. The rest of the cockpit was hard to get a good picture of as it is all black!




Next in the instructions was to buld the engine that is on the front of the plane. Most of it cannot be seen in the finished plane!




The small piece that completes the front of the plane was very difficult to put in as it has another part underneath, that you can't see in the picture, which kept falling off and disappearing into the plane!




Now the nose of the plane is completed with a part that fits over the engine, which hides it all!




Adding the wings to the model make it seem more like a plane


Here are the wheels and bomb that are glued onto the bottom of the plane. The wheels are optional, you can choose whether you would like to have them out or inside the plane. It is easier to not add them but if you do not buy a seperate stand for the plane and want to display it, adding the wheels makes the plane look better. The undercarriage on this plane is quite long compared to the other planes that I have built, making them even more delicate than they normally are.




Here are the wheels and bomb attached to the bottom of the plane. They were very difficult to glue on and would keep falling forwards or backwards.




Next I glued on the propellers and other tiny details and finally glued in the pilot. I then painted the area that surrounds the cockpit so I could glue it on.




I painted the lines on the cockpit before I glued it onto the plane as I found it sightly easier as I didn't have to work around the whole plane.




I then finished painting the plane, following the instructions on the back of the box




As the wheels and propellers were very delicate, I sat the plane on top of the four paint pots that came with the kit while the paint was drying. It was quite difficult to make sure that the pots were holding the plane up and not just sitting underneath the wings as the wheels were the same size as the pots!
Luckily, this paint did not take long to dry and it did not need many coats to give the plane an even colour as it was a matte paint. I found that paints that give the model a shiny finish need many more coats than matte paints, like the paint for the Folland Gnats in my hangar


Here is a picture of the painted plane, ready for the final decorations, the transfers




Here is the finished model. In the first picture above you can see the saucer that had a small amount of water in to remove the transfers from the paper they were on. A cocktail stick and sometimes the small peice of paper that is in the box is helpful to put the transfers in the right position. When moving the transfer when it is on the plane, pull it with a cocktail stick from the front to avoid the transfer from crinkling.
